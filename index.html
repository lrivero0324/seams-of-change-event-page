Our Mission

Seams of Change is an interactive exhibit that exposes the environmental and ethical costs of fast fashion. Through immersive storytelling, data, and design, it challenges visitors to rethink their clothing choices and embrace sustainable alternatives.

From excessive water usage and carbon emissions to textile waste and microplastic pollution, the fast fashion industry is one of the most damaging forces in modern consumer culture. Yet most people remain unaware of how their clothing choices fuel this crisis.

Your Journey Through the Exhibit

"Life of a T-Shirt" Journey

Walk through a timeline tracing a basic T-shirt's lifecycle—from fossil fuel extraction to mass production, international shipping, and eventual landfill or incineration.

Microfiber + Pollution Zone

Experience a live demo showing how synthetic clothing sheds microplastic fibers in the wash, using magnification and water tanks to reveal what's usually invisible.

Beyond the Tag

Scan digital tags on displayed garments to reveal the full environmental cost and labor journey behind everyday fashion.

Patch-Making Workshop

Participate in a creative corner where visitors design fabric patches to express sustainable fashion goals or statements.

Fast Fashion Landfill Wall

A striking visual display of what 11 million tons of annual U.S. clothing waste looks like—scaled to show the 70 lbs discarded per person.

Worker Stories

Learn about the human cost: 9 in 10 garment workers are not paid a living wage. Hear their stories and understand the true price of cheap fashion.
